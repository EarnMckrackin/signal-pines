# Signal Pines — Game Bible (Lite)

Condensed from `SIGNAL_PINES_DESIGN_PLAN.md`. That file is canonical.

**One line:** A teenage skater in a strange 1986 Pacific Northwest town uses
skateboarding, analog gadgets, and improvised toy-like tools to uncover a secret
lab experimenting on local teens.

**North star:** *Signal Pines is about kids using movement, friendship, analog
tools, and forbidden routes to uncover the adult world's hidden map.*

**Core fantasy:** "I know the line nobody else sees."

## Pillars

1. **Skate Everywhere** — the board is identity, transport, escape, expression.
2. **Kids Know the Real Map** — adults see roads and fences; kids see rails,
   rooftops, drains, and shortcuts.
3. **Investigate With Analog Tools** — camcorder, walkie, VHS, payphones, microfiche.
4. **Outsmart, Don't Outgun** — stun, distract, trip, escape. Never a soldier.
5. **The Town Gets Stranger at Night** — curfew, fog, lab vans, signal anomalies.

## Setting & cast

**Cedar Rift, Oregon, 1986.** Dying timber town; Northstar Research is its uneasy
lifeline — and is abducting "resonant" teens who can perceive a signal buried
beneath the ridge.

- **Player** — customizable teen skater, 16–17. One default background for now.
- **Eli Voss** — best friend, filmmaker, conspiracy kid. Disappears in The Mouth.
- **Rina Park** — tech brain. Radios, scanners, RC car mods.
- **Cal Mercer** — skate legend, rival-turned-ally. Knows the secret routes.
- **June Bell** — school paper editor. Connects disappearances across decades.
- **Sheriff Vale** — knows more than he admits. Not a cartoon villain.
- **Dr. Kael** — Northstar lead. Believes the experiments prevent something worse.

## Vertical slice — "The Mouth" (10–15 min)

One evening: skate tutorial on Mercy Hill → Zip's crew scene → Main Street line →
ride to the Spillway → film Eli's trick (first camcorder glitch) → enter The Mouth
→ stealth past Northstar moving a sedated teen → tunnel chase with signal
distortions → white blast, Eli gone → skate home under curfew → bedroom VHS
playback: impossible footage of Eli. *"If you're seeing this, they already know
about you."*

## Tone rules

- Warmth before fear. Start grounded, then get strange.
- Adults are morally complicated, not cartoon evil.
- Analog texture everywhere: VHS, static, hand-drawn maps, tape labels.
- Toy-weapons are stylized fiction — never real construction details.
